INTUS

Contradiction-First Diagnostics for Regulatory Text
Pipeline Overview
Use case: Detecting semantic tensions between regulatory documents—old vs new law, guidance vs label, policy vs SOP—in pharmaceutical, medical device, and compliance domains.

What It Does

  • Flags meaning conflicts between clauses across documents
  • Does not resolve contradictions—preserves tension for human review
  • Surfaces cross-document tensions typically obscured by summarization workflows

How It Works

  • Signal Detectors detects semantic relations: entailment, contradiction, neutrality
  • Contradictions become persistent objects, not transient errors
  • Clustered into thematic pressure regions for systematic review

What You Get

  • Ranked conflict pairs with full clause traceability
  • Cluster view organized by theme (timeline, access, governance, responsibility)
  • Exportable markdown and HTML reports for audit documentation
  • Negative control checks to detect over-triggering

Diagnostic only (non-generative). INTUS does not generate conclusions or enforce resolutions. All flagged contradictions trace to specific detector judgments and source text segments. Human reviewers retain full control over interpretation and action.

Current Status

A working demonstration was completed in January 2026 using EU veterinary pharmacovigilance regulations as a testbed. The system successfully detected cross-document tensions that traditional compliance workflows would likely miss.

A full technical manuscript describing the architecture, design invariants, and reproducibility guarantees is in preparation for arXiv.
