What Is INTUS?

INTUS is a research program exploring how artificial systems can detect, preserve, and reason about semantic inconsistency without forcing premature resolution or belief collapse.

Unlike consistency-enforcing AI pipelines, INTUS treats detected contradictions as diagnostic signals: structural indicators of epistemic strain that can be accumulated, tracked, and inspected over time.

The goal is not to replicate human cognition, but to design and study transparent, modular epistemic architectures that expose their internal tensions, reasoning boundaries, and failure modes.

Research Focus

Our work investigates several interconnected questions:

  • How contradictions can function as first-class epistemic signals
  • How reasoning systems can preserve inconsistency without explosion
  • How hypotheses emerge and stabilize under epistemic pressure
  • How structured memory alters long-term reasoning dynamics
  • How diagnostic architectures can bound generative systems

Domains of Interest

Initial experimental domains include:

  • medicine and clinical reasoning
  • law and regulatory interpretation
  • scientific discourse and hypothesis revision
  • epistemic robustness and AI interpretability

Development Status

INTUS is an exploratory research prototype developed from first principles. A working demonstration focusing on cross-document contradiction detection in regulatory texts was completed in January 2026.

A full technical manuscript describing methods, ablations, and reproducibility guarantees is in preparation for arXiv.
