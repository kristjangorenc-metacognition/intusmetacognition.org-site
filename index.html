About the lab

Zavod INTUS explores new approaches to artificial reasoning and cognitive modelling. Our focus is on the foundations of understanding: how systems detect inconsistency, update beliefs, integrate knowledge, and maintain coherence over time.

The lab operates at the intersection of cognitive science, computational reasoning, philosophy of mind, and AI safety. Our work is currently in an early research phase, with active development of internal prototypes and methodological tools.

Our mission

INTUS investigates how an artificial system might detect contradictions, generate hypotheses, integrate them into memory, and track how those internal structures evolve. The aim is not to replicate human cognition, but to design a transparent, modular, and logically grounded epistemic engine that can explain its steps, expose its internal tensions, and update its conceptual structure through cycles of self-evaluation.

Current large-scale AI architectures are strong pattern generators, but they seldom represent epistemic coherence explicitly. They typically do not track how beliefs emerge, how they interact, or how they should be revised when confronted with conflict. INTUS approaches reasoning as a regulated feedback process: tensions in input trigger hypothesis generation, hypotheses are tested against accumulated memory, and accepted insights reshape the system’s conceptual space.

Theoretical motivation

The project is informed by epistemology, cognitive systems theory, cybernetics, and the study of how minds manage uncertainty. The working hypothesis is that intelligence depends not only on recognising patterns, but also on forming structured internal representations, evaluating their coherence, and updating them when they conflict with experience.

INTUS develops computational tools for detecting semantic tension, tracking conceptual drift, resolving contradictions, and constructing explicit justification structures that reveal how beliefs depend on and influence one another. In this view, knowledge is treated as a living system: something that must remain organised, interpretable, and internally consistent across time and across domains.
